# Document Requirements Examples

This document shows what documents the automated system will request based on different borrower profiles and loan scenarios.

## üìã Always Required (All Loans)

These documents are required for **every** loan application:

1. **Driver's License or State-Issued ID** - Government-issued photo identification
2. **Loan Application (Form 1003)** - The application form you just completed
3. **Credit Authorization** - Permission to pull your credit report
4. **Loan Estimate (LE)** - Summary of loan terms, rates, and costs
5. **Intent to Proceed** - Confirmation you want to move forward
6. **Borrower Certification** - Your signature authorizing the loan process
7. **IRS Form 4506-C** - Authorization for lender to request tax transcripts from IRS
8. **Bank Statements** - Last 2-3 months of checking and savings account statements
9. **Appraisal Report** - Property valuation (lender orders, but you pay)
10. **Title Report** - Shows property ownership and any liens
11. **Homeowner's Insurance** - Insurance quote or binder for the property
12. **Flood Certification** - Determines if property is in a flood zone

## üíº Employment-Based Document Requirements

### Scenario 1: W-2 Employee (Traditional Employment)

**Example:** You work for a company and receive a W-2 at tax time

**Documents Requested:**
- ‚úÖ **Pay Stubs** - Most recent 30-60 days (showing year-to-date income)
- ‚úÖ **W-2 Forms** - Last 2 years (2023, 2022)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Tax Returns (1040s)** - Last 2 years (if self-employed income, rental income, or other income sources)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Employment Verification Letter** - Letter from employer confirming job title, salary, and employment dates

**Why:** W-2 employees have stable, verifiable income. Pay stubs show current income, W-2s show annual income history.

---

### Scenario 2: Self-Employed / Independent Contractor

**Example:** You're a freelancer, consultant, or run your own business (receive 1099s)

**Documents Requested:**
- ‚úÖ **Personal Tax Returns (1040s)** - Last 2 years with all schedules
- ‚úÖ **Business Tax Returns** - Last 2 years (Schedule C if sole proprietor, or business returns if LLC/Corp)
- ‚úÖ **Year-to-Date Profit & Loss Statement** - Current year income/expenses
- ‚úÖ **Bank Statements** - Last 3-6 months showing business revenue deposits
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Business License** - If required in your state/industry
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **1099 Forms** - If you received 1099s from clients

**Why:** Self-employed borrowers need to prove income stability through tax returns and P&L statements since there's no employer to verify.

---

### Scenario 3: Business Owner (LLC, Corporation, Partnership)

**Example:** You own a business (LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp, Partnership)

**Documents Requested:**
- ‚úÖ **Personal Tax Returns (1040s)** - Last 2 years
- ‚úÖ **Business Tax Returns** - Last 2 years (Form 1120, 1120S, 1065, etc.)
- ‚úÖ **Year-to-Date Profit & Loss Statement** - Current year business performance
- ‚úÖ **Balance Sheet** - Business assets and liabilities
- ‚úÖ **Business Bank Statements** - Last 3-6 months
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Business License/Registration** - Proof business is legally registered
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Partnership Agreement** - If partnership
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Corporate Bylaws** - If corporation

**Why:** Business owners need both personal and business tax returns to show how business income flows to them personally.

---

### Scenario 4: Retired

**Example:** You're retired and living on retirement income

**Documents Requested:**
- ‚úÖ **Social Security Award Letter** - Shows monthly benefit amount
- ‚úÖ **Pension Statements** - Monthly pension payment documentation
- ‚úÖ **Retirement Account Statements** - 401(k), IRA, 403(b) statements
- ‚úÖ **Tax Returns** - Last 2 years (to show retirement income)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Annuity Statements** - If you receive annuity payments
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Investment Account Statements** - If using investment income

**Why:** Retirees need to prove stable retirement income sources that will continue for at least 3 years.

## üè¶ Loan Type-Based Requirements

### Conventional Loan

**Example:** Standard loan not backed by government

**Additional Documents:**
- ‚úÖ **Down Payment Verification** - Bank statements showing funds for down payment
- ‚úÖ **Reserve Funds** - Typically 2-6 months of mortgage payments in reserves
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) Application** - If down payment is less than 20%

**Why:** Conventional loans have stricter requirements but often better rates for qualified borrowers.

---

### FHA Loan (Federal Housing Administration)

**Example:** Government-backed loan with lower down payment requirements

**Additional Documents:**
- ‚úÖ **FHA Case Number** - Assigned by lender
- ‚úÖ **FHA Appraisal** - More detailed than conventional appraisal
- ‚úÖ **Down Payment Source** - Can be gift funds (with gift letter)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Upfront Mortgage Insurance Premium (UFMIP)** - One-time fee
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Monthly Mortgage Insurance** - Required for life of loan if <10% down

**Why:** FHA loans are more lenient on credit and down payment but require mortgage insurance.

---

### VA Loan (Veterans Affairs)

**Example:** Loan for veterans and active military

**Additional Documents:**
- ‚úÖ **Certificate of Eligibility (COE)** - Proof of VA loan eligibility
- ‚úÖ **DD-214** - Military discharge papers (if not active duty)
- ‚úÖ **Statement of Service** - If active duty
- ‚úÖ **VA Appraisal** - VA-specific property requirements
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Funding Fee Documentation** - VA funding fee (can be rolled into loan)

**Why:** VA loans don't require down payment or PMI, but need proof of military service.

---

### USDA Loan (Rural Development)

**Example:** Loan for rural properties

**Additional Documents:**
- ‚úÖ **USDA Eligibility Certificate** - Property must be in eligible rural area
- ‚úÖ **Income Verification** - Must meet income limits for area
- ‚úÖ **USDA Appraisal** - USDA-specific property requirements
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Guarantee Fee** - USDA guarantee fee (can be rolled into loan)

**Why:** USDA loans are for rural properties and have income limits.

---

## üè† Property Type-Based Requirements

- ‚úÖ Title Report
- ‚úÖ Homeowner's Insurance
- ‚úÖ Flood Certification

---

### Condo / Townhouse

**Additional Documents:**
- ‚úÖ **HOA Documents** - Homeowners Association bylaws, budget, insurance
- ‚úÖ **HOA Financial Statements** - HOA's financial health
- ‚úÖ **HOA Insurance Certificate** - Master insurance policy
- ‚úÖ **Condo Questionnaire** - Lender-specific form about the condo association
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Special Assessment Documentation** - If any pending assessments

**Why:** Condos require HOA approval and documentation since you're buying into a shared community.

---

### Multi-Family Property (2-4 Units)

**Additional Documents:**
- ‚úÖ **Rental Income Documentation** - If you plan to rent out units
- ‚úÖ **Lease Agreements** - If units are currently rented
- ‚úÖ **Schedule E** - From tax returns showing rental income/expenses
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Property Management Agreement** - If using a property manager

**Why:** Multi-family properties can generate rental income, which needs to be documented.

## üí∞ Asset-Based Requirements

### If You Have Investment Accounts

**Documents Requested:**
- ‚úÖ **Investment Account Statements** - Last 2-3 months (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)
- ‚úÖ **Brokerage Statements** - Showing account value and holdings

**Why:** Investments can be used as reserves or to show financial stability.

---

### If You Have Retirement Accounts

**Documents Requested:**
- ‚úÖ **401(k) Statements** - Current balance and contribution history
- ‚úÖ **IRA Statements** - Traditional or Roth IRA account statements
- ‚úÖ **403(b) Statements** - If applicable
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Retirement Account Withdrawal Documentation** - If using retirement funds for down payment

**Why:** Retirement accounts show long-term financial stability and can be used as reserves.

---

### If Using Gift Funds for Down Payment

**Documents Requested:**
- ‚úÖ **Gift Letter** - Signed by gift giver stating it's a gift, not a loan
- ‚úÖ **Donor's Bank Statements** - Showing they have the funds
- ‚úÖ **Transfer Documentation** - Proof funds were transferred to your account
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Donor's Tax Returns** - Sometimes required to verify source of gift funds

**Why:** Lenders need to verify gift funds are truly gifts and not loans that need to be repaid.

- ‚úÖ **Homeowner's Insurance Policy** - Current policy information
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Home Equity Documentation** - If doing cash-out refinance

---

### Cash-Out Refinance

**Additional Documents:**
- ‚úÖ **Purpose of Cash-Out** - Explanation of how you'll use the funds
- ‚úÖ **Current Mortgage Payoff Statement** - Exact amount needed to pay off current loan

**Why:** Lenders need to know why you're taking cash out and how you'll use it.

## üéØ Key Takeaways

- **W-2 Employees:** Need pay stubs + W-2s + tax returns
- **Self-Employed:** Need tax returns + P&L statements + business bank statements
- **Business Owners:** Need personal + business tax returns + business financials
- **Conventional Loans:** Standard requirements, may need reserves
- **FHA Loans:** Lower down payment, but requires mortgage insurance
- **VA Loans:** No down payment, but need military service proof
- **Condos:** Require HOA documentation
- **Multi-Family:** May require rental income documentation

The system automatically adjusts the checklist based on your specific situation!
